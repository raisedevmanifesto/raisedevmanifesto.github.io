These core values guide all design and implementation decisions for the RAISE Manifesto website. They provide a framework for evaluating options and ensuring alignment with our vision.

Core Values

The following values guide the creation, stewardship, and evolution of the RAISE Manifesto website:

Simplicity

Strive for clarity and ease in both design and communication. Complexity is only added when it serves genuine purpose.

Implications:

  • Minimize dependencies and technical complexity
  • Prefer clear, concise language over jargon
  • Design with a focus on core content, not decorative elements
  • Create a straightforward information architecture

Transparency

Make decisions, processes, and changes visible and understandable to all. Openness builds trust and invites collaboration.

Implications:

  • Document the rationale behind significant decisions
  • Make source code and artifacts publicly accessible
  • Clearly attribute contributions and influences
  • Communicate changes and updates openly

Humility

Recognize that no methodology or artifact is perfect. Remain open to feedback, revision, and learning from others at every stage.

Implications:

  • Acknowledge open questions and areas for improvement
  • Regularly revisit and refine artifacts
  • Welcome diverse perspectives and constructive critique
  • Balance conviction with openness to change

Bravery

Have the courage to question assumptions, invite critique, and pursue bold ideasu2014even when they challenge the status quo.

Implications:

  • Challenge conventional development practices
  • Propose and test innovative approaches
  • Embrace measured risk in pursuit of important goals
  • Stand firm on core principles when they matter

Accessibility

Design and develop to meet high accessibility standards, ensuring the content and experience are available to everyone, regardless of ability or technology.

Implications:

  • Aim for WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance at minimum
  • Prioritize a world-class experience for screen reader users
  • Test with diverse users and assistive technologies
  • Consider accessibility from the beginning, not as an afterthought

These values were established at project inception and are referenced during all decision-making processes. They evolve as we learn and grow.
