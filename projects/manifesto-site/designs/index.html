These Design Decisions document the visual and interaction choices that shaped the RAISE Manifesto website. They translate our intention and values into tangible user experience elements, ensuring consistency and purpose.

Design System & Visual Language

Typography Choices

Typography forms the foundation of our design system, setting the tone for the entire experience while ensuring accessibility and readability.

Primary Font: Open Sans

Used for body text, navigation, and UI elements to ensure clarity and readability.

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. (Regular 400)

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. (Semibold 600)

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. (Bold 700)

Rationale: Open Sans was selected for its exceptional legibility at various sizes across devices, its clean and neutral character that doesn't distract from content, and its complete character set supporting multiple languages.

Secondary Font: Lora

Used for headings and key statements to provide a distinctive, authoritative voice.

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. (Regular 400)

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. (Semibold 600)

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. (Bold 700)

Rationale: Lora provides a perfect balance to Open Sans with its slightly more formal, serif characteristics. It adds gravitas to headings while maintaining excellent readability, and its subtle details enhance the hierarchy without overwhelming the design.

Color System

Our color system is intentionally minimal, focusing on readability and contrast while creating a calm, focused reading environment.

Primary Colors

Background
#FAF9F6

Text
#333333

Rationale: The off-white background (#FAF9F6) reduces eye strain compared to pure white, while the dark gray text (#333333) provides strong contrast without the harshness of pure black, aligning with our accessibility value.

Accent Colors

Link Blue
#0056b3

Link Hover
#004085

Rationale: Our link colors follow conventional web patterns for immediate recognition while ensuring WCAG AA+ contrast ratios against our background colors. The slightly darker hover state provides clear feedback for interactive elements.

Layout Principles

Our layout system embodies the simplicity value while creating clear hierarchy and focus on content.

Content Width & Readability

Main content areas are constrained to a maximum width of 800px for optimal readability, with appropriate padding on smaller screens.

Rationale: Research indicates that line lengths of 65-75 characters are optimal for reading comprehension. Our content width constraint ensures text remains within this ideal range on larger screens while adapting appropriately to smaller viewports.

Whitespace Hierarchy

We use a consistent pattern of whitespace to establish hierarchy: more space before elements than after them, creating clear grouping and separation.

Rationale: This pattern creates natural visual grouping, helping readers understand the relationship between different content sections without requiring explicit visual borders or background changes, supporting our simplicity value.

Responsive Approach

The site is designed with a mobile-first methodology, ensuring the experience scales appropriately across all devices.

Key Breakpoints

  • Base/Mobile: Up to 640px
  • Tablet: 641px - 1024px
  • Desktop: 1025px and above

Rationale: Rather than targeting specific devices, our breakpoints are chosen based on content needs. The design starts with the essential content experience for small screens and progressively enhances layout and navigation as screen space increases.

Navigation Adaptation

Navigation elements adapt based on available screen space, with a simplified vertical organization on mobile that transforms to horizontal layouts on larger screens.

Rationale: This approach ensures navigation remains accessible at all sizes while making efficient use of screen real estate, prioritizing content while maintaining wayfinding context.

Accessibility Guidelines

Accessibility is not an enhancement but a core requirement of the RAISE Manifesto site, in alignment with our core value of accessibility.

WCAG 2.1 AA Standards Target

The site aims to meet Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 AA standards through the following design principles:

  • Perceivable: Information and user interface components must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive
  • Operable: User interface components and navigation must be operable
  • Understandable: Information and the operation of user interface must be understandable
  • Robust: Content must be robust enough to be interpreted by a wide variety of user agents

Implementation Focus: The site is built with semantic HTML structure, meaningful heading hierarchy, and intentional color choices to support these principles.

Inclusive Design Approach

The following design patterns guide our approach to accessibility:

  • Semantic HTML: Using appropriate elements that communicate meaning (nav, main, section, etc.)
  • Progressive Enhancement: Ensuring core content is available to all users regardless of technology
  • Content Structure: Clear information hierarchy with logical reading order
  • Typography Choices: Text that maintains readability across different sizes
  • Breadcrumb Navigation: Clear wayfinding cues for all users

Rationale: These patterns create a more inclusive foundation that benefits all users, not just those with disabilities, demonstrating our commitment to human-first design as expressed in our core principles.

These design decisions were established at project inception and guide all visual and interaction implementation to maintain consistency and purpose.
