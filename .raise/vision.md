# Vision: RAISE Manifesto Site

## What are we trying to do?
We are creating a simple, accessible website to publish the RAISE Manifesto—a story-first methodology for meaningful, artifact-driven software engineering—and to invite open, thoughtful commentary from the world.

## Why does it matter?
The RAISE Manifesto is a call to rethink how software is envisioned, built, and evolved. By sharing it publicly, we aim to:

- Spark dialogue about story-first engineering and artifact-driven alignment.
- Empower visionaries, technical and non-technical alike, to bring their ideas to life.
- Invite critique, questions, and contributions to help the methodology grow.

## Who is it for?
- Makers, founders, and engineers seeking a more purposeful way to build.
- Non-technical contributors who want to participate deeply in shaping software.
- Anyone curious about the intersection of narrative, values, and technology.
- People who have always had ideas, but have struggled to bring them to life.

## What change do we seek?
- To make the RAISE Manifesto accessible, visible, and alive through public feedback.
- To foster a community of reflection and co-creation around story-first engineering.
- To demonstrate that methodology itself can be a living, evolving artifact.

## Core Values
- Clarity: The site should make the manifesto easy to read, understand, and respond to.
- Openness: All are welcome to comment, critique, and contribute.
- Integrity: Comments and discussions are moderated to uphold respectful, constructive dialogue.
- Iteration: The manifesto and site will evolve in response to feedback and new ideas.

## Success Criteria
- The manifesto is published in a clear, readable format.
- Visitors can easily leave comments or questions.
- The site encourages thoughtful engagement and signals openness to revision.
- The process for updating the manifesto and responding to feedback is visible and transparent.

## Out of Scope
- Advanced features (user accounts, voting, rich media, etc.)—the focus is on simplicity and dialogue.
- Broad content beyond the manifesto and its discussion.
