# Draft User Stories

These stories are under consideration for future iterations of the RAISE Manifesto site. They are not yet committed to the current implementation.

> **Note**: Some of these stories were previously in the main stories list but have been moved here as they await implementation.

---

## Cosign the RAISE Manifesto

**As a** supporter of the RAISE Manifesto,

**I want to** add my name to a public list of cosigners,

**So that** I can show my support and signal alignment with its principles.

### Acceptance Criteria
- There is a clear way for visitors to express support (via email, form, or automated system).
- Cosigners’ names (and optional messages/links) are displayed on the site.
- The process is simple, transparent, and respects privacy/consent.
- The feature can be added in a future iteration without disrupting the current simplicity.

---

## Methodology Integration

**As a** technical leader,

**I want to** understand how RAISE methodology integrates with existing development practices,

**So that** I can incorporate it gradually into my team's workflow.

### Acceptance Criteria
- Comparison with other methodologies (Agile, etc.)
- Guidance on incremental adoption
- Recommendations for team roles and responsibilities
- Discussion of common challenges and solutions

---

## Evaluating Project Success

**As a** project manager,

**I want to** know how to measure the success of RAISE implementation,

**So that** I can evaluate its impact on our development process.

### Acceptance Criteria
- Suggested metrics for measuring methodology effectiveness
- Warning signs of methodology drift
- Case studies or testimonials from other teams
- Guidelines for continuous improvement

---

## Expanded Example Projects

**As an** interested visitor,

**I want to** explore more real-world examples of RAISE implementation,

**So that** I can understand its practical application across different project types.

### Acceptance Criteria
- Expanded gallery of example projects in diverse domains
- Clear categorization and filtering options
- Complete artifact sets for each example
- Progressive journey from vision to implementation

---

## Contributing to Artifacts

**As a** domain expert,

**I want to** contribute effectively to RAISE artifacts,

**So that** my knowledge is accurately captured in the development process.

### Acceptance Criteria
- Templates for non-technical contributions
- Guidelines for effective collaboration with technical team
- Examples of successful cross-functional input
- Clear language without technical jargon

---

## Display Author Information

**As a** reader of the RAISE Manifesto,

**I want to** understand who is behind the manifesto,

**So that** I can know the context of its creation and the perspective of the author(s).

### Acceptance Criteria
- The site includes a clearly identifiable section for "Who We Are" (author bio)
- This section provides a short bio as specified in the design document (e.g., brief, text-only, 2-4 sentences)
- The presentation of this section is de-emphasized compared to the main manifesto content, adhering to the site's design principles for modesty and simplicity
- The section is located on the main page, after the manifesto text and before the "What's Next" section, accessible via scrolling

---

## Provide Discussion Links

**As a** reader of the RAISE Manifesto,

**I want to** easily find links to external discussion threads,

**So that** I can join the conversation and engage with other interested individuals.

### Acceptance Criteria
- The site includes a clearly identifiable section for discussion links
- Links to specified external discussion platforms (e.g., Reddit, Substack, or others as provided) are present and functional
- The section is located at the very end of the page, after all other content, accessible via scrolling

---

## Future Plans Section

**As a** reader of the RAISE Manifesto,

**I want to** learn about the specific future plans related to the RAISE methodology,

**So that** I can see where it's headed and understand its development roadmap.

### Acceptance Criteria
- The site includes a clearly identifiable section called "What's Next" (future plans)
- This section specifically outlines plans for developing applications and tools using RAISE
- The section includes 2-4 specific bullet points about upcoming developments
- The presentation emphasizes concrete next steps rather than open questions
